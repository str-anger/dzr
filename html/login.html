Rules ▼
This game engine is inspired by DozoR ([en], [ru]). There are usually a few stages. The goal of each stage is to solve puzzles in limited time and then find a code hidden somewhere in real world. Answer to the puzzle is usually a location in predefined area (town, neighbourhood, region). After solving a puzzle, you should come to this location and find an alphanumeric code. Enter this code in the input field to complete the stage.

Puzzle example
Example puzzle for a game in Dublin (example of predefined area):
And now draw a rectangle around this church. Ok, now try to follow the sun, but do not cross the line! Didn't you know you may even see Chelmsford from here?

Solution:
Look for weird things. (Rectangle = Bar, Church = Temple) = Temple Bar. Temple bar is the area on the south bank of river Liffey. "Follow the sun" = go west. Chelmsford is the main city of Essex. So, you should go to the west of Temple Bar, and search on the intersection with Essex street.

Code
The code may be written on a wall, or may be encoded with an object (e.g. if you see T34 tank or BMW X5 in front of you, guess the code).

One location (stage) may have multiple hints which will open with time on the page. Refresh the page to get hints and time updates. If time is up, you will be sent to the next stage. If all stages are finished, you will receive final instructions (e.g. assembly point).

Complexity
Each task has a complexity, consisting of a number (1,2,3) and optional "+". Here "1" means code is on the ground level, in obvious place. "2" means a bit of climbing (1 floor, slide on a playground). "3" means physical skills required, may be dangerous. "+" means "hidden, hard to find".

Complexity examples:
  • 1+: under the bench in small letters
  • 2: under the bridge, on the pillar
  • 3+: under the bridge, but over the water
